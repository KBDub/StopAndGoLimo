## Rule

When describing the Stop & Go service area in general service pages, hero descriptions, and marketing copy, always use language that includes ALL directions of the Chicago metro — not only the southwest suburbs where the company is headquartered.

**Why:** The client flagged that listing only "New Lenox, Naperville, Joliet, Plainfield, Bolingbrook, Frankfort, Mokena, Orland Park" is not inclusive of the greater Chicagoland area and excludes potential clients in the north, northwest, and west suburbs.

**Chicagoland city groups from docs/seo/ChicagolandCities.pdf:**

- **West & Far West:** Aurora (~181k), Naperville (~153k), Joliet (~152k), Elgin (~115k), Bolingbrook (~75k), Schaumburg (~76k), Downers Grove (~50k), Elmhurst (~46k), Carol Stream (~39k), Geneva (~22k)
- **North & Northwest:** Waukegan (~89k), Arlington Heights (~76k), Palatine (~68k), Skokie (~65k), Des Plaines (~61k), Mount Prospect (~54k), Crystal Lake (~40k+), Elk Grove Village (~32k), Gurnee (~31k), North Chicago (~31k), Highland Park (~30k), Algonquin (~30k)
- **South & Southwest:** Cicero (~84k), Orland Park (~59k), Tinley Park (~57k), Oak Lawn (~57k), Berwyn (~54k), Maywood (~23k)

**How to apply:**
- General service pages: "throughout Chicagoland and all of Illinois" or "from the southwest suburbs north through Arlington Heights and Waukegan, west through Elgin and Schaumburg, and into the city of Chicago."
- When listing specific examples, mix in at least one north/northwest or west suburb alongside southwest suburbs.
- City-specific pages (e.g., the Channahon page) focus on that city but say "all of Chicagoland" not "southwest suburbs" when mentioning broader coverage.
- Acceptable compact phrases: "throughout the Chicago metropolitan area," "all of Chicagoland," "across Chicagoland and northern Illinois."
