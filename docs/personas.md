# Top 5 Percent — Customer Personas

## Brand Personality

Each persona page should feel written for that specific person — not a generic audience. Use plain language at a 7th-grade reading level. Lead with their pain point, then pivot to the outcome Top 5 Percent delivers. Never use all-caps, em dashes, or corporate-speak.

**Recommended component:** Use `x-ui.card-detailed-info` when building editorial content pages targeted at a specific persona. Pass persona-relevant images as `image1` and `image2`, write the `$intro` slot to address their primary need, use `$mid` for supporting context and use cases, `$lower` for practical how-to or logistics, and `$footer` for local closing copy with internal links. Wrap the first 4 words of the `$intro` first paragraph in `<span class="text-olive font-semibold">` to create a branded lead-in.

## 1. The Trades Hustler

**Miguel, 34** — independent plumber/HVAC tech, Joliet/Romeoville area

Running a one- or two-man operation, his truck is his storefront. He needs door lettering, a magnetic sign, maybe a partial wrap — yesterday. Budget-conscious, no time for back-and-forth, values same-day service heavily. He's not buying luxury, he's buying leads on wheels. The ROI framing of the vehicle graphics page speaks directly to him.

**Key needs:** Speed, simplicity, same-day service, clear pricing
**Primary products:** Vehicle graphics, door lettering, magnetic signs, partial wraps
**Messaging angle:** Your truck is your billboard — make it work for you

## 2. The Family Reunion Organizer

**Denise, 52** — matriarch-type, deeply community-rooted, South Side Chicago / south suburbs

She's coordinating 80+ people for a summer reunion and needs matching custom shirts that actually look good. She wants rhinestone or foil options, she has opinions about colors, and she's probably been burned by a cheaper shop before. The specialty materials breadth and local brick-and-mortar presence give her the confidence to trust Top 5%.

**Key needs:** Quality assurance, specialty options (rhinestone/foil), local presence, reliability
**Primary products:** Custom shirts, rhinestone apparel, foil prints, group orders
**Messaging angle:** Don't settle for cheap — your family deserves the best bling around

## 3. The School Spirit Coordinator

**James, 41** — athletic director or booster club chair, Plainfield or New Lenox

Ordering spirit wear in bulk — hoodies, shirts, possibly banners for the gym. He needs to manage multiple SKUs, sizes, and tight event deadlines. The free web store offering is a massive draw — it offloads order management entirely. He'll become a repeat customer every season if the first order goes smoothly.

**Key needs:** Bulk ordering, deadline reliability, size management, web store convenience
**Primary products:** Custom hoodies, spirit shirts, gym banners, online store setup
**Messaging angle:** Easiest spirit wear season you've ever had — we handle the logistics

## 4. The Boutique Brand Builder

**Aisha, 27** — launching a small clothing or lifestyle brand, Joliet area

She has a concept, a logo (or she needs one), and a dream of selling her own apparel. The Top 5% Clothing and Black Doll brand lines signal to her that this isn't just a print shop — it's a place that gets brand identity. She'll start with a DTF transfer run, test the market, and scale. The DIY online designer lowers her barrier to entry significantly.

**Key needs:** Low minimums, design support, DTF transfers, brand identity understanding
**Primary products:** DTF transfers, custom apparel, design services, small-batch runs
**Messaging angle:** Start your brand here — we built ours the same way

## 5. The Small Business Owner Leveling Up

**Rick, 48** — owns a landscaping or cleaning company, Crest Hill or Lockport

He's been in business 10 years on word-of-mouth alone and is finally ready to look professional. He needs yard signs, a vehicle wrap, maybe window decals for his new office space — the full kit. He doesn't want to coordinate five vendors. The one-stop-shop positioning is exactly his pain point solved, and the Veteran-owned badge earns his immediate respect.

**Key needs:** One-stop shop, full business identity package, professionalism, veteran respect
**Primary products:** Yard signs, vehicle wraps, window decals, banners, branded apparel
**Messaging angle:** One shop, everything you need to finally look as good as your work

## 6. The Event & Nonprofit Coordinator

**Sandra, 39** — church event planner or nonprofit program director, Will County

She's ordering banners, table runners, and event shirts on a shoestring budget with a hard event date. She needs creative flexibility (she may only have a rough idea, not finished artwork) and a shop that won't nickel-and-dime her for design help. The approachable, consultative tone of the site — plus quantity discounts — makes Top 5% feel like a partner rather than a vendor.

**Key needs:** Budget flexibility, design consultation, deadline certainty, quantity discounts
**Primary products:** Event banners, table runners, event shirts, printed materials
**Messaging angle:** We're your creative partner, not just your printer
